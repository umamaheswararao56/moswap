How can you determine the Condition of your device?

It is very important to correctly rate the physical and functional condition of your device. These ratings are critical for helping MoSwap to quote the best offer for your device.

You can determine the condition of your device by using the description for the following condition types:

Smartphone Conditions:

Broken (if ANY of the following are true)
Cracked screen or body Doesn't power on or make calls Missing buttons or parts Broken or cracked hardware
Fair (if ALL of the following are true) Major dings and dents on the body Heavy scratches on display Powers on and display works normally All buttons and ports are fully functional
Good (if ALL of the following are true) No cracks on screen or body Powers on and makes call No major scratches or scuffs All buttons and ports are fully functional
Flawless (if ALL of the following are true) No scuffs or scratches, looks like new No cracked screen or body Everything works perfectly

Conditions Section For Laptops:

What condition is your product in?

1. Broken Your laptop is in BROKEN condition if ANY of the following are true: Does not power on Multiple functional defects (i.e., the hard drive, keyboard and trackpad are all defective; multiple ports are non-functional, etc.) Display is heavily damaged or non-functional Power port is broken or non-functional Scratches, cracks or severe dents System fan does not function Display does not function properly OS cannot be installed
2. Fair Your laptop is in FAIR condition if ALL of the following are true: Non-functional or missing component but still functions otherwise, such as keyboard, port, trackpad, hard drive, or optical drive (if there are multiple non-functional components, please choose Broken.) Moderate to heavy scratches, dents or scuffs Moderate to heavy wear on the keyboard and/or trackpad Display functions properly Minor damage to the display hinge Minor display issues, such as dead pixels or screen burn OS can be/is installed
3. Good Your laptop is in GOOD condition if ALL of the following are true: 100% functional (if your item has a functionality issue, please see the Fair condition option) Normal wear on the keyboard and/or trackpad Light scratches and/or very minor dents or nicks Very light scratches or scuffs on the display surface, which do not affect viewing OS can be/is installed
4. Flawless Your laptop is in FLAWLESS condition if ALL of the following are true: Works perfectly Looks like new out of the box No noticeable flaws No scratches or wear on the body No scratches or scuffs on the display surface OS can be/is installed

How to deactivate "Find My iPhone" before shipping to MoSwap?

"Find My iPhone" is a feature for iPhone, iPad, and iPod Touch that can help locate your device if lost, but can also block activation by a new owner. Because of this, MoSwap cannot offer full trade-in value for devices with Find My iPhone enabled.

To ensure a fast and hassle-free process, please make sure to turn off Find My iPhone before sending it to MoSwap.

Just follow these simple steps to turn off "Find My iPhone":

Step 1: Locate and tap the "Settings" icon on your device's home screen.



Step 2: Tap "iCloud" in the Settings menu.



Step 3: If Find My iPhone is ON, tap the slider to turn it OFF.



If you are prompted to for a password, enter your iCloud password and tap "Turn Off" to confirm.

Please note: If you have already send your gadget to us and forgot to turn off "Find My iPhone", then please see the "How to deactivate Find My iPhone after shipping to MoSwap" FAQ and follow the steps to reset the setting remotely. Please contact us if you need any assistance with this process. We are always there to help you!

How to turn off "Find My iPhone" after I have shipped it to MoSwap?

Find My iPhone feature, if not turned off, will automatically block activation by other people. Due to this, MoSwap is unable to find a new owner for your device. Also, MoSwap will not be able to offer full trade-in value for the devices in which "Find My iPhone" feature is enabled.

You can still turn off "Find My iPhone" even after sending your device to us. Just follow these simple steps to turn off "Find My iPhone" remotely:

Step 1: Go to http://www.icloud.com/#find and login using your Apple iCloud account (this is the same account you use on your device.)



Step 2: Click "Devices" at the top, and locate the device you sent to moswap. After a few moments, iCloud will finish updating the location on each of your devices and the device you sent to moswap should appear as offline. Click on the device to select it, go back to the device list, and then click the "X" button to remove it from your account. Please note: device must be powered off.



Step 3: Click "remove" on the next screen to confirm.

If you received an email from MoSwap asking you to complete these steps, use the link in your email to let us know you've removed it from your account so we can continue to process your device.

You can also visit Apple's support website (https://support.apple.com/kb/PH2702?locale=en_GB ) to turn off "Find My iPhone".

Please note: If you are still in possession of your device, you can turn off "Find My iPhone" right from the settings menu. For further help, please see the "How to deactivate "Find My iPhone" before shipping to MoSwap" FAQ and follow the steps to remove the device from your iCloud account. Please contact us for assistance. We are always there to help you!
